In 2050, the climate status will be defined by the behavior of the different countries and governements in the battle against global warming during the first half of the century. The different climate scenarios are expressed as RCPs (Representative Concentration Pathways) associated with a value representing the radiative forcing (how the global earth temperature will increase due to greenhouse gases emissions). For example the scenario RCP 2.6 corresponds to a forcing of +2.6W/m2, the higher the radiative forcing is, the higher the greenhouse gases emissions will be in the future.

These RCPs are paired with different Share Socioeconomic Pathways (SSP) describing trends that could shape the future society. Once again multiple plausible solutions are proposed.

We are going to see the effects of 5 different scenarios on the food production in 2050 compared to the one in 2000:

  • SSP1-RCP2.6 (Sustainability): Low challenges to mitigation and adaptation: The world gradually changes to a more sustainable economy and consumption model oriented toward a lower ressource and energy use.
  • SSP2-RCP4.5 (Middle of the road): Medium challenges to mitigation and adaptation: Slow progress on the road of sustainable consumption mode, the countries do not make progress envenly toward a more eco-friendly economy.
  • SSP3-RCP6.0 (Regional rivalry): High challenges to mitigation and adaptation: Competition between countries and regions leads them to a higher material consumption, their development is self oriented rather than at a broader level so inequalities persist. The population tends to stabilize in industrialized countries but is highly increasing in developing ones.
  • SSP4-RCP6.0 (Inequality): Low challenges to mitigation but high challenges to adaptation: The gap between wider between developed countries and the ones with low incomes. The countries tend to focus on a local environmental policy that is around middle and high classes issues.
  • SSP5-RCP8.5 (Fossil fuel development): High challenges to mitigation, low challenges to adaptation: The focus is fully on competitve markets and technological progress. The developement of human capital allows to trigger succesfully the environmental challenges even with a greedy enerfy lifestle around the world.
